AuthZ vs AuthN

AuthN (Authentication) = it is the process of verifying the identity of a user or system. It confirms that the user or system is who they claim to be. This process typically involves presenting credentials, such as a username and password, and validating them against a database or authentication service. Authentication ensures that only authorized users gain access to resources or functionalities within an application.
AuthZ (Authorization) = it is the process of determining what actions or resources a user is allowed to access within an application or system. Once a user's identity has been authenticated, authorization specifies the permissions and privileges associated with that identity. Authorization mechanisms enforce access control policies, ensuring that users can only perform actions or access resources that they are authorized to. Most common access is CRUD which stands for Create, read, Update & Delete.
Auth0? = It is a platform that abstracts away much of the complexity of implementing authentication and authorization, offering developers ready-to-use solutions that can be easily integrated into their applications.

Hands on MongoDB and Mongo-Express

docker-compose image for MongoDB 

# Use root/example as user/password credentials
	version: '3.1'
	
	services:
	
	  mongo:
		image: mongo
		restart: always
		environment:
		  MONGO_INITDB_ROOT_USERNAME: root
		  MONGO_INITDB_ROOT_PASSWORD: example
	
	  mongo-express:
		image: mongo-express
		restart: always
		ports:
		  - 8081:8081
		environment:
		  ME_CONFIG_MONGODB_ADMINUSERNAME: root
		  ME_CONFIG_MONGODB_ADMINPASSWORD: example
		  ME_CONFIG_MONGODB_URL: mongodb://root:example@mongo:27017/
